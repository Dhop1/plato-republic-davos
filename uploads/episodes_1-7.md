# **I. GLAUCON’S CHALLENGE — “NO ONE IS JUST ON PURPOSE”**

Glaucon isn’t satisfied with the polite ending of Book I. If you recall, they sort of knocked off the discussion at the end of book one, with sort of a shrug of the shoulder, and even Socrates admitting, heck, this whole thing about “justice” isn’t solved, not even a little bit.

Here’s the actual line:

Translation:
*Don’t give us feel-good philosophy. Prove it.*

Then he drops the first grenade.

Glaucon divides all goods into three types:

> **“There are three kinds of good:
>
> 1. things good in themselves, things like joy, laughter, music, friendship, These are the things we’d still choose even if they came with no perks — no rewards, no benefits, no payoff. They’re good just because they’re good.”
> 2. things good for their consequences, Things like going to the dentist, drinking a bitter, nasty medicine, or paying taxes. These are the broccoli of life — we don’t crave them, we tolerate them because the consequences are better than the alternative.”
> 3. and things good both in themselves and for their consequences.”**health, learning, knowledge and courage fall in this category—-This is the sweet spot — the stuff that’s good on the inside and good on the outside. It feels right, and it produces a life that works.” *(357b–c)*

II. PART 1: THE PRISON (THE SETUP)
So how can I get this through in the best way (((paaause—long pauuse)))) I want you to close your eyes for a second. (Unless you’re driving, then please don't close your eyes—just focus your mind.)
I want you to build this movie set in your mind.
Socrates asks us to imagine a cave, deep underground. Dark, dank, cool, maybe water drops dripping from the ceiling. 
Inside this cave, there are prisoners.
But they aren't just visiting. They have been there since childhood. They were born, grew up, and lived there from birth….there is nothing else, there is no concept of life, but the “cave.” 
And they are restrained. Their legs are chained. Their necks are shackled so tightly that they cannot turn their heads. They can only look forward at the back wall of the cave. They only see what is directly in front of them—that is their reality. They know nothing different. 
Now, behind the prisoners—way up behind their backs—is a fire burning.
And between the fire and the prisoners, there is a raised track—like a puppet show stage.
And walking along this track are people—Puppeteers.
They are holding up objects: stone and wooden statues of men, animals, and trees.
Now, do the physics.
The fire shines on the objects. The objects cast Shadows onto the wall in front of the prisoners. So the wall, in essence, is like a movie theatre screen. 
And because the cave echoes, when the Puppeteers speak, the prisoners think the Shadows are talking…. In their limited perspective, the only “real” things in life are what they see on the cave wall—the sights and the sounds and the smells of the cave that is their reality, there is nothing else. 
Socrates asks the devastating question:
"Would they not suppose that they were being addressed by the shadows passing by them?"
To these prisoners, the Shadows are not "fake." The Shadows are the only reality they have ever known.
If you told them about a "Real Tree," they would think you were crazy. A "Tree" is that dark, flat thing on the wall. It is tall, green, or rustling in the wind. It is a grey shadow, dancing on the screen from the firelight. 
THE QUOTE (515c):
"To them, the truth would be literally nothing but the shadows of the images."
Dr. Hopkins’ Translation:
So if we pause, you and I, right here, and I ask you…..Who are the prisoners? Who are the prisoners in this story?  
I mean an obvious answer would be those sorry, pathetic souls who have a warped sense of reality. 
But if we slow down, slow down and think about it—-They are us. In essence, across human civilazations, the majority of the people alive today and those billions and billions that have already lived and died….well, collectively, we are the slaves. 
This isn't a story about ancient Greece. This is a story about Black Mirror.
The "Wall" is your iPhone screen.
The "Shadows" are the Instagram posts, the 10-second news clips, the memes, the outrage loops. The ideological brainwashing by the corrupt system of politicians, the materialism of the postmodern world… all of them, casting shadows across our mind, our lives, our hopes, dreams, fears, and aspirations-all these things bombarding us constantly from cable news anger factories to social media materialism to AI deep fakes….
We sit there, chained to the algorithm, staring at the wall for 8 hours a day in jobs we don’t like to impress people we don’t even know. We scream at the shadows (Twitter fights—political debates). We fall in love with the shadows (Influencers, materialism, power, money, career, opinions). We vote based on the shadows. We go into debt based on the shadows, we break friendships or families up over the shadows….and we don’t even bat an eye. 
And notice the Puppeteers.
The shadows aren't natural. They are manufactured. Made up. Someone is holding up the stick. Someone is curating the feed… yet the anger, frustration, and anxiety are never (or rarely) directed at the true puppeteers, but at each other. 
We think we are seeing the world, but we are seeing a puppet show designed to keep us staring at the wall. Keep up pacified, keep us “content”, keep us under control—we don’t break the chains, we stare at the screens “entertaining us” with, with, well, nothing. In essence, we are not much better, really, than those chained to their spot inside the cave. 
III. PART 2: THE TURN (THE PAIN)
So, what happens next?
Socrates says: Imagine one prisoner is freed. Why? Who knows, maybe the puppetteers were running some sick experiment to see what would happen if such a dumb, brainwashed human actually got to see reality. 
His chains are struck off. He is forced to stand up and turn around.
Now, Hollywood would tell us this is a triumphant moment! The cinematic, Braveheart- or Rocky-style music swells. He’s free! He’s free!  
But Socrates says, “Oh, know, that isn’t what actually happens.” No. It is Agony.
* Physical Pain: His muscles have been atrophied for 20 years. Standing up hurts.
* Visual Pain: He turns toward the fire. His eyes have never seen light, real light.…all he knows is the darkness.  The glare burns him. He is blinded.
He can't see the puppets. He can't see the fire. It’s all a blur of pain.
And then one of puppeteers or controlling humans tells him: "Hey, look. Those things are real. The shadows were fake."
What do you think, Does our freed prisoner believe them? Does he reach this moment of enlightenment, thrusting him to a brave, new world. No! Actually the opposite happens. 
He thinks the person is lying. He thinks the Shadows were clear and distinct (because his eyes were used to them). They were comfortable, safe, and non-threatening. 
But the reality, ooooh the Reality looks blurry and painful.
He wants to run back to his chains. He wants to go back to the comfort of the lie. Our Rocky or Braveheart superhero is, well, actually, average—we seem to see him as almost pathetic, at least weak. From the book, it says, 
THE QUOTE (515c):
"And if he is compelled to look straight at the light, will he not have a pain in his eyes which will make him turn away and take refuge in the objects of vision which he can see?"
The Psychology:
This is Cognitive Dissonance.
When you present someone with facts that destroy their worldview, they don't say "Thank you." Their brain registers it as a physical attack…..I know we all like to think that when presented with information that conflicts with our worldview, well, we are all open minded, critical thinkers and make unemotional pivots from our stated view. 
I’m sorry, but in general, that is BS, total BS—-think about it, anytime your “teams” political party is found on the wrong side—do you automatically and with humility say, oh, yes, I was wrong, and now I see the light—-or, do you say—-fake news!!! Deep fake!!! Yes, but their side did this; they are still worse. Oh, well, the only reason my side did X is that that evil side did Y….or no way, my side is wrong….the other side is ignorant because they get their cable news for X and I, the smart one, get my news from Y….   I mean, that’s enough, right?  You get the picture—the fact is, most of us, most of the time, when faced with cognitive dissonance, will use whatever means necessary to retreat back to our own preconceived views of the world. 
Waking up isn't fun. It hurts. That’s why most people prefer the coma. That’s why Cypher in The Matrix wants to go back and eat the fake steak. (If you saw the Matrix), And if you haven’t, my goodness, do yourself a massive favor and watch the Matrix — the first one, not all the sequels. 

IV. PART 3: THE ASCENT (THE RUGGED PATH)
But Socrates doesn't let him go back into his cave and his old life. 
He says the prisoner is "dragged by force" up the rough and steep ascent, out of the cave.
He is kicking and screaming. Oh yeah, he wants nothing to do with the change—sort of like that diet you started or that exercise routine or that new years resolution that well, you just didn’t really want to do, well, this poor guy is that multiplied by 1000. 
Finally, he is dragged out into the Sunlight.
And now he is totally blind. The Sun is too bright.
He has to look at things in stages:
1. First, he looks at shadows and reflections in the water 
2. Then, he looks at the objects themselves—real trees, real flowers, real rocks…just real things in general. 
3. Finally, after a long time, after his eyes finally adjusted fully, he can look up at the Sun itself.
And he feels the sun and realizes: Oh my God. The Sun causes the seasons. The Sun causes the years. The Sun is the reason the shadows in the cave even existed.
He realizes that everything he cared about in the cave—the awards for "Best Shadow Watcher," the political fights over which shadow was faster—it was all meaningless. None of it was real—he is stunned (finally) and excited (finally), and Joila—the moment of transformation has arrived!  We thought we would get this right away, but it took a while, it took literally being dragged out of the cave, but finally, finally, he sees “Truth.”
And He says he would rather be a slave on earth than a King in the Cave.
He is finally Awake.

V. PART 4: THE RETURN (THE DANGER)
This is where the movie usually ends. The hero finds the truth and lives happily ever after.
But this is Plato.
Socrates says: He must go back.
We didn't educate him for his own happiness. We educated him to save the City. We need him now, to return, to bring the new knowledge and cause a revival in the city—to wake them all up!!!!
So, the new philosopher, new enlightened person…goes back down into the cave with wisdom far beyond the capacity of everyone, but he is enthusiastically ready to share it all.
But here is the tragedy.
When he walks back into the dark... he is blind again.
His eyes are used to the Sun. He can't see the shadows anymore.
He stumbles. He looks awkward.
The prisoners—who are still chained—look at him.
They challenge him to a "Shadow Guessing Game" (Current Events/Politics).
And he loses. He looks like an idiot because he doesn't care about the shadows anymore.
The prisoners laugh at him.
They say: "Look at him! He went up, and he came back with his eyes ruined. The ascent is bad for you. Critical thinking is dangerous."
And then Socrates drops the darkest line in the book.
He asks: "If he tries to free them... and lead them up... what will they do to him?”
So if Socrates asked YOU that question, how would you answer?  If our ‘hero’ of the study tries to free them… and lead them up…what will they do to him?
Well the answer comes in the book, 
THE QUOTE (517a):
"And if they could lay hands on the man who was trying to set them free and lead them up, they would kill him."
"They would kill him."
Socrates isn't guessing here.
Remember, Socrates was actually a real person. He tried to free Athens. He tried to tell them the shadows were fake.
And what did they do? They voted to execute him. 
They killed the man who tried to unchain them. Socrates, in his own life, rather than bow down to the corrupt laws of the state…chose to die, by his own hand, by drinking Hemlock as directed by the jury in his trial. Wow, that’s believing in who you are and what you are about—— very few in history can make that claim. …. 
But this is where we get to the Prophet’s curse. 
The Prophet’s Curse.
This is Cancel Culture in its deadliest form.
Society does not thank the person who tells the Truth. It attacks them. Because the Truth insults the Shadow. The truth disrupts the status quo, the truth forces the masses into uncomfortable situations.

It’s hard. It’s lonely. And people might hate you for it.
But as Socrates says: "Better to be poor and awake than rich and dreaming.”
